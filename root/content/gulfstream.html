About the Gulf Stream

Recorded history and the impact of the “Gulf Stream” dates to the original explorers of Columbus, Cabot and Ponce de Leon in the late 15th century. As the Spanish Conquest radiated from the Caribbean, the “Great Current” became the established route for exploration and settlement. After the Spanish, the British, French and Dutch used the stream to navigate the New World.

Benjamin Franklin was the first to thoroughly analyze and map the Gulf Stream during his travels as Ambassador to France onboard U.S. mail ships bound for Europe. Using handheld devices of his own design, he discovered the depth and temperature variations which truly defined it as a “River in the Ocean.”

For over five centuries, the “The Blue God” has constantly served sailors crossing the Atlantic and remains to this day a vital element of international commercial trade routes.
